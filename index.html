I am an aspiring entrepreneur and independent financial researcher.

I am currently focused on building 100 Doors LLC, an early-stage real estate management venture in the South Suburbs of Chicago. In addition to managing properties, I conduct data collection and analysis on local housing markets to understand changes in market conditions and identify potential investment opportunities. I am currently pursuing my broker’s license to deepen my understanding of the industry.

I believe economic research is a conduit for advantageous investment strategies and the forces that shape markets, consumer welfare, and long-term economic outcomes. My research interests include monetary policy, bond markets, real estate, and flagship ETFs. A subset of my work aims to uplift young investors by investigating how financial tools can build wealth.

I graduated with a bachelor’s from Haverford College with a major in Economics and a minor in Mathematics. Outside of academics, I participated in projects dedicated to economic research and impact investing, where I further honed my analytical skills and applied them to real-world problems.
